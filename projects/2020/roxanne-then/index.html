Anaesthetic Architecture:

What is the role of Architects in a Increasingly Digitalised World? 

 

Summary

Society is constantly changing, and with it, the role of Architects and purpose for Architecture constantly shifts accordingly Through analysing trends in both present day reality as well as Sci-fi, this thesis re-imagines a near future where urban experience is becoming more and more localised due to technological improvements which has resulted in a hybridised reality. 

Cities and technology present a dichotomy in how they operate as a social network - a tension between collective identity and hyper-individualisation. This presents Architects with a new materiality - of refresh rates and resolution, to reimagine architecture and especially so - the purpose of the public realm, and therefore the design of this piece of infrastructure in supporting both the individual and domestic living, through a digital and physical system that employs the use of overlay and masking.

About Me

Roxanne Then is a Master's Graduate at the Singapore University of Technology and Design. She believes that Architecture is a means to ask questions, and a way to critique the way we currently live, and can live. Hence, she believes in design that is derived from a compelling concept and narrative, one that challenges conventions and re-thinks the potentials of what makes a great design.

Roxanne believes that design often takes on an interdisciplinary nature, and therefore has worked across multiple  cales during her time as an Architectural Intern at Gensler and K2LD. In order to broaden her perspectives, Roxanne was part of the 2017 Grad Show Design team and has taken up multiple research and teaching roles during her time in SUTD. During her gap year, Roxanne did a short docentship at the 2018 Venice Biennale, and Interned at Think Tank Studio, where she worked on multiple small-scale exhibition designs. Most recently, she was also the Website Lead for the Young Women's Leadership Connection (YWLC).

Cities as Social Networks

Cities, in their high density nature, created the first form of social network. This was made possible through the random encounters formed whilst people traveled from place to place as part of their daily routines On top of that, certain typologies of architecture (such as the bath house) were communal and nodal in quality, contributing at a smaller scale, to be a social network in itself.

A Changing Reality:

Technology as Social Networks

Unlike physical spaces, Technology offers us the convenience of being in the comforts of our own homes, while still being able to be connected to people through the internet. The extent of this connection is also a lot more than the physical. The success of technology in doing so lies in its ability to emulate aspects of the physical, and sometimes replicate and even replace the fucntion of the physical as well.

Of course, the extent of which cities are able to function as a social network changes with time, and is dependent on the conditions present in that reality.

A Changing Reality: Through Retroactive Lenses

Changes within the city occur at 3 different scales - of the individual, collective and societal, all of which has influence over the other, with the extent of which it is able to do so, creating the cycle of change. Using the example of the Industrial revolution, the Arcades and the Flaneur, we will then use this lenses to speculate a near future, based on the conditions and technological improvements we see today and how it will influence the society of the future.

A Changing Reality: Through Sci-fi Lenses

An analysis done on various black mirror episodes reveals how sci-fi predicts technology will proliferate into society and be part of the everyday environment. It speculates that the reality of our future will be a hybrid one – where digital information is embedded in the physicality of things. It presents a dystopian future, where the implementation of technology and the effects of hyper-personalisation results in the eventual isolation or disconnection of the individual from reality, both of which has lasting impact on the collective identity and the way society operates.

The New Narrative : A New Materiality

Technology usage has been on the rise, and there has been an increase in demand for new spatial experiences. Whilst the current use of such technology on spaces remain small scale, sci-fi predicts how technology will proliferate into society through the use of autonomous robots as well as invasive embedded technologies, that forms a hybridised reality - one where digital information is embedded in the physicality of things, through the new materiality of refresh rates and resolution via the technique of overlays and masking.

Bringing it back to reality, this new narrative consists of the vast improvements to the logistical capabilities of autonomous robots, which has further pushed us towards the convenience of home, and how it will continue to be and become an even more integral part of our livestyles.  

Where the idea of the destination is no longer as important, and we live in an increasingly localised, and curated space, there is a need for public infrastructure to support such a lifestyle, and therefore  be redesigned, to one that has adapted to the new materiality of digitial overlays and masking, that of which will be vital in forming a healthy collective identity, especially in a changing society.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Intervention : The Digital Algorithm

The speculated near future is one where technology has developed to a point where the destination is no longer as important as our world has become a lot more localised. Public infrastructure such as roads and pathways have become redundant, and needs to be reesigned to support the individual and domestic living. There will be two parts to the intervention - a digital system, as well as a physical system to support it.  

The intervention will aim to be a framework that will be applied to one test site, and forms a framework for how the other 'islands' can be designed as well.  

The intervention assumes 2 main digital entities - our lenses and the autonomous drones that run the system. In order to support them, easy identification of different kinds of codes becomes important in affecting what they parse over to our Lenses.            

 

 

 

 

 

 

Intervention : The Physical System

The codes are applied to the physical system and hence, surfaces such as walls, floors, and ceilings become key information for the machines to translate information and sustain the system on the ground plane.By processing and communicating with other lenses and drones to parse accurate geo tagging information, the lenses then quickly curates different pathing in real time for which the human can choose to take, depending on their personal preferences, and availability of space at that point in time.

And Unlike system based objects as well as static surfaces to be overlaid on, human beings will be overlayed or masked according to the scenarios given.The design starts from a single hexagonal module that exist as different types, texture, variations and as props themselves, overall creating a journey system for people to navigate in the ground plane.

Multiplicity Through Aggregation

At the larger scale, The aggregation of the stages combined with flat ground form a larger stage that covers the island like random noise, creating meaningful spaces of different cluster areas for both individuals and group settings.

This aggregation also allows for the same journey to be experienced in different locations, No matter the start point and end point - people can be in the same ‘space’ at the same time by being digitally present in each other’s overlay even if they are in different locations.

The clustering of the stages with type, variation and texture, along with the built environment can also create interesting scenarios. Previously, hard edges defined programs and functions of spaces. However, the application of the intervention on the ground plane and sheltered conditions of the void deck make for a slightly different, yet still seamless immersive environment.
