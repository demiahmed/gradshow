Neurosensorium: The Architecture that consoles

Summary


Neurosensorium is the product that came out from the research on phenomenology, neuroscience and architecture. The research involves the understanding of the relationship between our senses, emotions and perception in order to bridge and apply neuroscience knowledge to architectural design. Neurosensorium is designed with consideration of grief emotions, material perception and traditions of burial spaces. It is not only a space catered for crematory and urn storage purposes, but also for contemplative, healing and remembrance purposes.

Thesis Statement: To create an architecture that consoles through the sensory medium without downplaying the idea of death. One that provides a space for contemplation and remembrance, in addition to its original function and without its existence being seen as intrusive.

Thesis Mentor: Bige Tuncer


About Me


Jingwen is a Masters student in Singapore University of Technology and Design. She is generally interested in designing interior, residential and mixed-use buildings.

"I believe that the environment we live in will affect our well-being and ultimately shape us. Hence, i decided to pursue something related for my thesis. While searching for a potential thesis topic, I read books about Cities, Evolution, Brain psychology, Our sensory organs and topics relating to Phenomenology, Intuitive design and Architecture. That was when i chanced upon the term "Neuro-Architecture" which became the seed of my thesis. The challenge was to try to bridge the gap between neuroscientific studies of our mind and body, and architectural design. How can we use these studies to design sensitively?"

Site

The site selection was based on 3 main criteria:
1. To work on an existing plot of land allocated for burial and cultural purposes based on the 2020 URA Masterplan.

2. Unlike conventional burial spaces where they are casted somewhere unreachable or forgotten, being close proximity to transportation provides easy accessibility to the space which allows people to visit the place easily for remembrance, alleviation of grief and contemplative purpose.

3. The potential to design something less intrusive along the boundary between the living and the dead.

The site is located at the current Bukit Brown Cemetery. It is right at the boundary between the residential and the cemetery according to the 2020 URA Masterplan. Although the site is currently very inaccessible, the upcoming Mount Pleasant and Bukit Brown MRT will be within walking distance to the site. Currently the site is populated with low-rise private property, however, there are upcoming residential areas in the future. Residents residing nearby could also access it through a connecting staircase from the site, ideally, to a neighbourhood park.
