Metamall

Abstract

In 2020, following that of COVID-19’s exacerbation on the declining economy, not only retail but the rest of the economy was too in dire straits. Hence it is imperative that one should take all means necessary to safely be able to revitalize the economy. 

 

For the thesis design, the main goal was to supplement an existing mall with a secondary system to bring about growth in a relatively stagnant or regressing retail space. This is achieved through understanding of the existing Collective Form and the utilization of circulation linkages in the form of communal, composed open space that mediates in the existing stagnant system and connects and proliferates a growth model of Group Form as defined by Fumihiko Maki, to build on the existing nature of the malls.

History of Retail

Click on this link for an expanded history of retail internationally.

Retail in Singapore began as a localization of the Southern Chinese urban shop dwellings that immigrants brought in with them in the 1800s. During this time, other than shophouses, there would be more informal markets and bazaars too, set up by various immigrant vendors. In 1822, Raffles sought to formalize the retail typology by the formation of the Telok Ayer Market. As Singapore grappled with urban planning following the return of the country to the British and the rapid handover for independence, wet markets began to develop in a bid to improve sanitation and hygiene amongst these hawkers.

These measures were fulfilled through the forced tabula rasa created by the Singapore government, under the 1966 Land Acquisition Act (LAA) which allowed the State to acquire land for public purpose from private owners at below market prices. The rapid urbanization of Singapore meant too that numerous shophouses, villages and slums were cleared, and in their place sprung up residential and commercial spaces of high density.

As a retail space in comparison to what became of Gruen's ideology of the mall, malls within Asia are well integrated into the urban landscape, a more transitory space like the retail of eld. With the concept of connectivity to a central spine and intelligent, adaptive buildings at its core, Singapore had achieved what the Metabolists dreamed of in People's Park Complex and Golden Mile Complex and yet, said buildings were treated with disregard in today's society.

Metabolist Forms


C O M P O S I T I O N A L   F O R M
A series of individually tailored buildings perceived to function as and to serve a collective system/purpose, such as temple complexes in Japan. A static means to spatial organization in architecture.


M E G A   F O R M
A large frame in which all the functions of a city or part of a city are housed. Many diverse functions may be beneficially concentrated within the frame, and there is an implied correlation between an increase in frame size and utility in combination and concentration of functions of the components.


G R O U P   F O R M
A group of individual elements that are linked to each other by common purpose, which then signifies growth through a reciprocal, symbiotic relationship both in design and operation. The element of group form is oft the essence of collectivity, functionally, socially and spatially.

Interventions


T O   M E D I A T E
To connect intermediate elements or imply connection by spaces that demonstrate the cohesion of the masses around them. Constitues multitudes of other transitions.

T O   D E F I N E
To surround a site with a wall, such that there is a visual connection between the elements. A wall can be either oppressive, confining or pleasantly protective.

T O   R E P E A T
To introduce a common factor amongst all dispersed spaces in a design.

T O   M A K E   F U N C T I O N A L   P A T H
To arrange buildings or parts as a sequence of useful activity, then to further reinforce and propel the individual along said path to catalyse development along its course.

T O   S E L E C T
To select a site with pre-established unity either by the physical or the visual, and to amplify the connection.
