URBAN FOREST BEYOND 2030, urban air mobility stimulated vertical city

Summary

Cities, under our usual impression, is rooted to the ground topography. Urban programs and infrastructure have always been developed horizontally. As rising population escalates the problem of land scarcity, some city planners already envision a future scenario where people reside in skyscrapers with all their essential needs fulfilled. With the development in construction technic and drone technology, future proof design thinking would be considering a possibility which is, could cities be planned and developed vertically?

Context

With rising population and limited land resource, Singapore has always been seeking to expand. One main approach to achieve this is by land reclamation. Over the past two centuries, Singapore's land area has expanded by 25%. However, land reclamation is actually an expensive approach to expand the ground territory. It also causes damage to marine life around Singapore. There will be a more sustainable and feasible option in the future for urban development.

Site

The site is selected to be four islands located at southwest of Singapore. These islands are distant from mainland of Singapore, and are currently undeveloped. Therefore, these islands have the potential to be developed into residential districts with the system of Urban Air Mobility. The four islands are namely 1. Semakau Island 2. Sudong Island 3. Pawai Island 4. Senang Island

The Question

The proposed reclamation plan for Semakau by the government is to expand the island to 4 times of its original area, however now with the possibility of vertical city developed with Urban Air Mobility network, is it still more feasible to reclaim land for future urban development? By looking at the most direct factor, economic factor, it can be seen from the cost estimation of both approaches that land reclamation is less feasible as a future method of continuous urban development.

Design Direction

The initial design direction is that instead of following horizontal layout of urban programs, the city is developed vertically and the programs are hybrid in the volume. Urban Air Mobility helps to achieve inner-building transport and inter-building transport, and also inter-island transport on a larger scale.

Reimagine Future Vertical City Structure

What could be different in future cities with Urban Air Mobility? In conventional cities, movement is mostly linear. Whereas in vertical cities, transport is node based, hence more flexible, also it provides more possibility for program distribution.

Reimagine Future Vertical City Structure

In a typical urban zone, programs are connected by linear transport, and are arranged in a grid layout, usually residential area takes more space and programs are condensed. In future scenarios, programs can be scattered and injected into the volumes with more variety, with Urban Air Mobility to carry people from point to point.

Massing Study

During the initial massing strategy exploration, Cellular Automata(CA) is used for massing volume iteration. The form generated by various steps of CA is translated from 2D to 3D. The result demonstrates great potential to accomodate the concept of vertical city and the flexible nature of Urban Air Mobility. Massing outcomes from various experiments show porosity and transforming spatial connectivity, which are essential elements to consider when designing a vertical city with drones flying through it.

Design

The original island of Semakau is divided into three parts, to maximize the efficiency of tansport, also to add in more variety to the programs on the ground level. Since building vertical structures upwards free up a lot of space on the ground, the ground is mostly covered with natural vegetation. Also the periphery is beach as a setback for coastal defense. There is a harbour for water transportation to and away from the island. Recreational hubs are allocated on the ground level, residents could enjoy nature and carry out recreational activities at the ground level. On the ground there are also sustainable design options, we have both water turbine electricity generator and wind turbine electricity generator. For transport, there is ground railway transport connect the base station of all three cities to the harbour. The vertical railway transport is an alternative transport to circulate throughout the vertical cities, with stations 500m distant from each other. For the cities there are ground foundations for basic support. Each city comprises 6 towers, centering the vertical rail transport. Due to different positions, the towers are designed to have varying height, to provide spatial characteristics and views towards surroundings.

Urban Structure

For urbun structures, modular design is proposed, as it is easier to install, and each individual element could work well with each other. For each of the individual modular cell, road is at the periphery, so that when adjacent cells combine together they form a road network. There are also support structures. Space at center is for buildings, building type can vary due to purpose of different programs. Not all cells are necessarily occupied by buildings, some are just support structures, to reduce weight and reinforce structural integrity, the space at center could be used for natural landscape. Public space, such as plaza is also created within cells surrounded by others. Also there is design for boarding and landing of drones.

Residential Zone

For residential module, on the ground there is electric automobile/PMD road, green belt and pedestrian walkways. Structural support ensures structural integrity. The design not only needs to take care of only one module, but more importantly ensures it works when modules are pieced together.

Workspace

For workspace modular design, future work environment is expected to be more flexible, the space is designed to have open floor plan, ideally to be shared by people from different industries. Various public space is provided, providing oppotunities for people to communicate and exchange ideas. There are external staircase for circulation and fire evacuation.

Retail

Retail is designed to be more vibrant. It is the connecting joint in the urban environment, the place for people to go shopping, enjoy food and socialize. Therefore, speaking of architectural elements, it is more visually appealing.

Park

Modular design of the park have the additional function to act as structural support. With or without the structural columns, the parks are great places to enjoy natural vegetation and relax.
