Summary

Overconsumption; A Global Issue



Overconsumption is a situation where resource use has outpaced the sustainable capacity of the ecosystem. A prolonged pattern of overconsumption leads to environmental degradation and the eventual loss of resource bases.



Stuck in a vicious cycle of consumption and production, we often consume absentmindedly at the expense of the environment. Unfortunately, a future based on our current trajectory is bleak and there is a need to normalise sustainable consumption amongst the everyday consumer society.



As such, this project aims to explore existing consumer mindsets and propose a new model for shopping experiences which includes educating consumers on existing issues in the production chain and introducing them to environmentally conscious methods of consumption and production

The Capital of Overconsumption

Orchard Road is known for being one of the pioneer shopping district in Singapore, lined with two rows of tightly packed malls, each brimming with a variety of shops. In recent years, it has also become home to flagship stores of multiple global fast fashion brands.



At the junction of Orchard and Grange Road sits the Orchard Building, home to the 4-storey H&M flagship store. Despite its roots in fast fashion, H&M has actively been taking steps to move toward the promotion of more sustainable consumption.



Their efforts have included collections designed with sustainable fabrics and customer rewards systems which provide incentives for shoppers to bring their unwanted clothes for recycling. With this foundation set in place, my project aims to redevelop the Orchard Building to create spaces which facilitate shopping beyond the purchasing of brand new garments.
