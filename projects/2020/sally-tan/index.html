volumes of bamboo

Summary

as opposed to the mainstream notion of construction as seemingly the stacking of building blocks, viewing architecture as a system of weaves, or rather a system of agglomeration, provides a different perspective on design. it presents itself as an alternative method of construction in architecture with much yet to be explored.


this thesis is interested in the potential of utilising agglomerative assembly systems in bamboo architecture and construction. through precedent analysis and a series of study models, a novel 3-step methodology of designing through agglomeration is put forth.


the proposed design sits on a site of natural bamboo growth and projected high urbanisation in the coming decade. with the burgeoning effects of climate change today, bamboo’s sustainability can help to mitigate pollution caused by the construction industry. this design project hopes to incorporate bamboo into an urban environment, to open up possibilities and shine a new light on this stereotyped ‘poor man’s timber’.

agglomeration process

the chosen methodology for working with bamboo in this thesis is agglomeration. this assembly process was chosen due to bamboo's naturally smaller scale. agglomeration is an assembly method that allows larger structures to be created from smaller parts.


3 case studies were first selected from a collection of projects that seems to exhibit agglomeration in their design. the chosen studies: yure by kengo kuma and associates, south asian human rights documentation centre by anagram architects and windshape by narchitects were analysed to get an understanding of how agglomeration is utilised in their design.


similarities from these 3 projects provided a framework that helped to give a more guided approach to agglomeration as a method of assembly.

proposed 3-step methodology

bamboo unit

applying the proposed 3-step methodology to bamboo, unit is first derived - a line and a curve. joinery selected is the traditional method of lashing, namely parallel, cross and orthogonal. due to bamboo's innate flexibility, lashing the material at one end while applying force at the other will result in bending of the material. resultant curvatures created are unit variations. these shapes cannot be achieved by the material on its own and they follow the concept of augmented materiality where they are unique to the bamboo material.


physical models are then built to explore possible curvatures that can be created from lashing bamboo and the spatial possibilities that result. it can be seen that different combinations of curvatures create vastly different atmospheres.


grasshopper3d is used to compile a catalogue of curvatures that can arise from lashing bamboo. though not entirely representative of the way bamboo bends, they serve as a point of reference for design later on.

bamboo system module



with the units, unit variations and joinery selected, 6 system modules are created using physical modelling. before they can be refined to form the final ensemble, the site is first chosen to determine the programmatic functions and hence specific design requirements. the 6 system modules will be incorporated into a singular massing on-site.

yunnan, china is identified due to its availability of bamboo and high projected growth rate in the coming years. with urbanisation comes an increased rate of construction and bamboo's sustainability can help in the fight against the burgeoning effects of climate change.


a rapidly developing region of yunnan is its capital, kunming where there is a strong emphasis on and celebration of their diverse cultures. bamboo crafts are also practiced in this region.


further zooming in, xishan is selected out of the 4 main city districts of kunming. this is a popular tourist destination with a mountainous topography. known for its scenic views, tourism in the area is focused within the mountains.


the specific site selected within xishan reflects the key feature of kunming - a city surrounded by mountains. the site is located at the intersection between a vehicular road and hiking trail, is surrounded by a forest and looks down to the city below.


entrance to the massing branches out from the existing site circulation. the massing looks down to the city centre along its right stretch and faces a bamboo temple down the mountains. bamboo-related activities will populate the massing to promote this local resource and existing culture. the proposal is imagined to be a test bed and showcase for the material

bamboo ensemble




from the design and functional requirements, each of the 6 modules are expanded upon and developed to form the final ensemble.


development of system modules


1: spatial enclosure. orthogonally-placed poles act as spines to define a capsule-like space below. following the same logic, the module is elongated to create a tunnel to hold selected exhibition works.


2: spatial enclosure. radial fanning of the bamboo opens up a space below. applied in the massing, this creates room for a cafe.


3: varying opening sizes. a smaller opening at the base expands to form a larger one at the top. this feature is emphasised and exaggerated on to create an atrium.

4: pinching and peeling. overlapping poles create a screen that hides and reveals selected activities. this is applied in the form of a facade.


5: branching. parallel lashing along the length of bamboo gives the idea of one pole splitting to form several. this is applied to create a wireframe enclosure for seating.


6: illusion of movement. the leaning form of the module adds dynamism to the design and this is applied along the circulation where the poles sweep upwards, enclosing a staircase.


connection details


all 6 bamboo volumes are incorporated within a singular concrete massing to show juxtaposition between materials and also their properties.


1: yunnan dragon bamboo is selected for use in this proposal as it is a local resource. pole diameter ranges from ~110-180mm and the plant rises to ~25m high.


2: both chopped and treated poles along with growing bamboo are utilised to aid in regeneration and sustainability of bamboo volumes. poly twine or nylon twine used to lash bamboos can be easily replaced in the event of degeneration.


3: weaved bamboo surfaces are incorporated to exhibit the multiple scales and uses of bamboo. transition from pole to weave is resolved by slicing the ends of poles to smaller strips to start the weave. more strips are then added. tri-axial kagome weave is utilised due to their higher in-plane shear stiffness as compared to that of quadrilateral shapes. this adds to the robustness of the design.


4: bamboo connect to concrete through steel pin joints. this allows for some movement instead of a completely rigid connection. direct insertion of bamboo into wet concrete will result in bamboo absorbing water from the concrete. excessive shrinking/swelling degrades the concrete and reveals steel connections below. this exhibits the different reactions of bamboo and steel to concrete.




application to building



all 6 bamboo volumes come together in a more familiar rectangular concrete frame. they contrast with the concrete by breaking through the regular grid. circulating through the building, users will come across and interact with the bamboo volumes in various ways. at different scales, heights and densities, they incorporate themselves into and add dynamism to the otherwise more rigid block. they take up multiple functions within the building and are key to the overall programmatic operations and experience of the building. incorporation of growing bamboo in the design results in a massing that is ever-changing and growing.




user interaction with bamboo volumes




inside the building, bamboo interacts with users in several ways. they can be categorised into 3 classes.


first, poles at various densities screens and filters views, hiding and revealing selected activities. as the thickness of poles are more human-scaled, they form a more intimate relationship with users. this is observed in f&b levels and along the ground level staircase.


second, thinner strips of bamboo are used to create weaved surfaces that can act as barricades or fabric-like walls. the varying weave densities filters light and creates peepholes for users to look into. this is observed in the atrium and tunnel of selected exhibition works.


third, bamboo is grown around the building and also in the central courtyard. this creates a more meditative space in the ground level. as bamboo grow, they curve in various directions and into the building, mingling with users on higher levels. while growing, they are more flexible and hence can be pre-bent for use in the bamboo volumes later on.




interior views




to end off, photo collages are created to reveal snapshots of what the building interior is imagined to be like.


the first view looks at the ground level where rows of bamboo crafts are on exhibition next to full poles encapsulating the staircase and living bamboo growing outside. the different scales, uses and stages of bamboo is put up on display.


the second view looks at the atrium space on the second level. amidst the various works on display, each bamboo volume also act as exhibitions themselves as they bring light and life into the building.
