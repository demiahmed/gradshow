To mitigate urban heat island in existing cities through the tactical introduction of water cooling strategies for minimal disruption with maximal effect.

Urban Morphology

Urban morphology plays a crucial role in UHI due to its influence on wind porosity. This can be seen through the large amounts of research that has gone into studying urban morphology and prescribing urban design guidelines to counter UHI. These is usually in the form of building and urban typologies relating to building shape, size and placement. However, while these prescriptions are effective for the designing of new cities, they do little in the improvement of existing cities, where changes can be difficult and costly. Mitigation strategies should be focused on improving existing built up cities to be more resilient.

Urban morphology provides useful insight into the microclimatic conditions in relation to the built environment. Data such as Ground Coverage Ratio (GCR) can be calculated to visualise the urban density at a higher resolution than generic numerical data across regions, while Frontal Area Density (FAD) provides an estimation of the pedestrian level wind conditions. Climatic data extracted from official weather stations provides a way to validate GCR and FAD visualisations prior to identifying areas of interests in relation to UHI.

Site Selection

Most readily available maps display density in the form of the number of people within the districts. However, when considering urban morphology, building footprint would provide a better estimation of wind flow and thus, UHI intensity. Hence, GCR and FAD visualisations provide better representations of UHI by considering the building's footprint. For this study, the maps are computed into a 500m grid utilising GIS data.

Data Extraction & Validation

Using a self-written python script, the data can be extracted from the official API by Data.Gov and its raw data processed and evaluated into monthly and yearly breakdowns. For FAD validation, site specific wind rose diagrams are generated using Rhino-Grasshopper and appended onto the FAD map to rationalise if the wind flow is in coherent with its surrouding environment.

The weather stations in close proximity with the CBD area are selected for this validation.

Design Concept


The design concept would be to include architecture into the water cycle. The water cycle describes the cyclic movement of water between the earth and its atmosphere. This movement has the potential to cool down the urban areas and mitigate UHI through its phase change. Integrating the building with the water cycle has may would allow the building to benefit from an additional passive cooling strategy.
